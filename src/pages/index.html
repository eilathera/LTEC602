Gamification is a very widely used and general term that blankets a variety of game types, structures, and delivery. We will be focusing on the basic delivery types of gamification in the classroom. In educational gamification, there are two type of delivery options.

First, is the physical delivery of instruction through a physical type of game (i.e board games, card games, etc.). The second type of gamification in education is through technology based delivery. These include games that run through computers, mobile devices, and game consoles. We will be focusing on technology based gamification. Below are some of the pros and cons about using these methods in the classroom.

Design Elements

The design of a computer based educational game is one of the most important things a designer needs to take into consideration. Here are 10 key components to making a successful game.

Self-Representation of Avatars

The game should allow students to create their own Avatars that represent them as a user. Whether that means creating a look-a-like character or just simple wardrobe changes based on user likes. A hint of personalization will go along way when creating a successful game that increases engagement.

Three-Dimensional Environments

Incorporation of a three-dimensional environment will get the user to become more engaged in the story as well as make the game more enticing for users as they explore the game world. The use of 3D graphics also increases game play due to users enjoying the “real world” experience.

Narrative Context

A good backstory, history of character, or even a storyline of missions gets users sucked into the game keeping them involved and interested in completing the task/mission.

Learning Styles

How we learn… There are three main learning styles that children show preference towards. Although, most students will be have tendencies in all three areas, we all have a dominant style that we gravitate towards. The three learning styles are as followed:




Visual

These learners use visuals to process information. They prefer using images, colors and maps to organize information and communicate with others.

Auditory

Hearing instructions read aloud help these learners more than reading written ones. They prefer to listen and hear information.

Tactile

Visual and auditory learning settings are difficult for these learners. They learn best through doing and learn best when there are hands-on, physical activities.

General Education Students

These student rely heavily on the teacher's ability to differentiate each lesson so that all students, regardless of ability, can understand the concept. This task is one that many, if not all teachers struggle with. Although it is very time consuming, teachers invest the time and effort to make visuals and plan activities that will be interesting to students. One drawback in this process is that editing these materials from year to year is difficult. Visuals may get outdated or students interests might shift making examples irrelevant.

Technology integration makes the process of planning shorter and more efficient. In combination with gamification, teachers develop a new teaching style and are able to accommodate all three learning styles. Gamification also allows students to interact through activities, work in groups, discuss topics, and increase workplace readiness skills. On top of those benefits teachers also have peace of mind that lessons can be revised very easily.

Special Education Students

Most times these students need more intensive assistance when learning due to their disabilities. The use of individualized instruction programs outlines specific things that teachers need to ensure continued progress in all areas of development (academic readiness, fine motor, self-help, social, etc.) Teachers in special education have to plan lessons that benefit all students in their own specific learning needs and styles.

Special education teachers plan and create individualized lessons focusing on each students needs. This can be very time consuming especially with classes sizes ranging from 4-12. The use of gamification can help teachers create lessons that are engaging and appeal to all students and their disabilities. These technology based lessons are editable making it very simple to modify content and difficulty for each student’s personalized needs.
