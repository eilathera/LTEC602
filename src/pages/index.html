Learning Styles

Visual

Visual learners prefer using pictures, images, colors, and maps to organize information and communicate with others. These learners use visuals to process information.

  • Sit near the front of the classroom (It won't mean you're the teacher's pet!)
  • Have your eyesight checked on a regular basis
  • Use flashcards to learn new words
  • Try to visualize things that you hear or things that are read to you
  • Write down key words, ideas, or instructions
  • Draw pictures to help explain new concepts and then explain the pictures
  • Color code things
  • Avoid distractions during study times

Auditory

Auditory learners prefer learning through listening and hearing information. Auditory learners do better when instructions are read aloud rather than having to read written ones.

  • Sit where you can hear
  • Have your hearing checked on a regular basis
  • Use flashcards to learn new words; read them out loud
  • Read stories, assignments, or directions out loud
  • Record yourself spelling words and then listen to the recording
  • Have test questions read to you out loud
  • Study new material by reading it out loud

Tactile

Tactile learners are those that learn best through touching and doing activities. These learners are the “hands on” learners and learn best when there are physical activities that help them understand concepts. These learners typically have difficulty attending to visual and auditory learner settings.

  • Participate in activities that involve touching, building, moving, or drawing
  • Do lots of hands-on activities like completing art projects, taking walks, or acting out stories
  • It's OK to chew gum, walk around, or rock in a chair while reading or studying
  • Use flashcards and arrange them in groups to show relationships between ideas
  • Trace words with your finger to learn spelling (finger spelling)
  • Take frequent breaks during reading or studying periods (frequent, but not long)
  • It's OK to tap a pencil, shake your foot, or hold on to something while learning
  • Use a computer to reinforce learning through the sense of touch

Students

General Ed

General education students all have different way of learning. These student rely heavily on the teacher's ability to differentiate each lesson so that all students, regardless of ability, can understand the concept. This task is one that many, if not all teachers struggle with. One way that general education teachers ensure that they are hitting all learner areas, is through time tedious lesson planning and material creation. Although, very time consuming teachers invest time and effort to make visuals and plan activities that will be interesting to students. A drawback of this, is there is no way of editing these materials from year to year. VIsual may get outdated or students interests might shift making examples irrelevant. One thing that technology integration does is make the teacher process of planning a lot shorter and more efficient. Technology, more specifically gamification, is a teaching style in itself. Teachers are able to hit all three learning styles through this medium of teaching. Gamification will also allow students interact with activities, work in groups, discuss topics, and increase workplace readiness skills. On top of those benefits teachers also have peace of mind that lessons can be tweaked or changes very easily.

Special Ed

Special education students also have many different ways of learning. These students most times do need more intensive assistance when learning due to their disabilities. The use of the IEP or individualized instruction program outlines specific things that teacher need to hit to ensure continued progress in all areas of development. (academic readiness, gross motor, fine motor, self-help, adaptive, social, etc.) Teachers in special education have to plan lessons that hit and benefit all students in their own specific learning needs and styles. Most time special education teachers will plan and create individualized lesson for each student focusing on their own needs. This can be very time consuming especially with some classes sizes ranging from 4-12 students/caseloads. One thing that technology integration does here is again making the teacher process a lot more efficient. The use of gamification in specific will help teachers make lesson that are engaging and appeal to all students and their disabilities. These technology based lessons will be easily editable making it very simple to modify content, and difficulty for each students personalize needs.
